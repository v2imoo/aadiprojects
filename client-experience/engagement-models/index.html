What it is

Scoped to the outcome, not the hours.

There is no single correct way to bring a firm like ours in. Some problems want a sharp, time-boxed build; some want a senior operator inside the team for a season; some want a standing seat — a fractional CFO or CSO; and some are consequential enough that we put our own capital and conviction alongside yours. The format follows the outcome, never a timesheet.

Whatever the shape, your Engagement Partner remains the constant, and we move between formats as the situation evolves — a build sprint that becomes an embedded role, a readiness program that leads to a principal partnership. It is one relationship with flexible delivery, which is precisely the advantage of working with a single joined-up firm rather than assembling a roster of vendors.

Here are the five formats, and the moment each is the right call.

The formats

Five ways to bring us in.

Engage one — most clients begin with the sharpest and expand from there.

Build sprint

A fixed-scope build that ships a working system, product, or model in weeks. Best when you need a specific thing built and live, fast.

Embedded operator

A senior operator sits inside your team and runs a function until it is self-sustaining. Best when you need capability now, not a plan for acquiring it.

Readiness program

We prepare you for what's next — a raise, a sale, a market entry, an AI rollout — and close the gaps before the event, not during it. Best when something big is coming.

Fractional capability

A senior CFO, CMO, CSO, CHRO, or CTO at full weight, part time. Best when you need the seat and the judgment, but not yet the permanent hire.

Principal partnership

We put skin in the game — capital alongside you, or our work for a share of the upside. Best when conviction runs high on both sides of the table.

Tailored blends

Most real engagements mix these and shift over time, with one partner flexing the model. Best when the need is evolving, which it usually is.

Flexibility

Tailored to your team, as you change.

A format is not a cage. As your situation evolves — you raise, you scale, you hit a wall — the engagement evolves with it. A build sprint can become an embedded role; a readiness program can become a principal partnership; a fractional seat can step up around a transaction and step back after it closes.

Your Engagement Partner is the constant through all of it, holding the context so you never start over with a stranger. That continuity is the quiet, compounding advantage of working with one accountable firm instead of stitching together a supply chain of specialists.

Questions, answered

The things people ask first.

Which format is right for us?

Usually clear after one discovery conversation. We'll recommend the sharpest starting point honestly — often a build sprint or a readiness program.

Can we change format later?

Yes — that's the design. The same partner flexes the model as your needs shift, without losing context or restarting the relationship.

What is a 'principal partnership'?

Where we believe, we put our own skin in — capital alongside you, or our work for a share of the upside instead of a full fee. Aligned incentives over assurances.

Do you offer fractional executives?

Yes — a senior CFO, CMO, CSO, CHRO, or CTO at full weight, part time, for as long as you need the seat and not a day longer.

Not sure which model fits?

Tell us the situation. We'll recommend the sharpest way to start — and flex it as you grow.
