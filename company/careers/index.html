Why here

A rare place to do broad, senior work.

Most firms ask you to choose a discipline and remain inside it. Aadi is built the other way. Because we advise, build, and invest under one roof, the people here move across the work — a strategist who helps shape a deal, an operator who joins a venture, an investor who sits inside a build. The breadth is the point, not an accident.

We are deliberately boutique, which means there is no pyramid to climb and nowhere to hide. Everyone sits close to real work and is trusted with real judgment early. If you are an operator weary of advising from a safe remove, or an advisor who wants to actually build, this is a place that will use all of you.

And we do it on a corridor most firms cannot credibly touch — which keeps the work varied, the stakes real, and the craft sharp.

Who thrives here

The people who do well at Aadi.

You've made things real

You've shipped a product, closed a deal, run a function, or started a company — and you want your hands in the work, not a safe distance from it.

You hold candor as a discipline

You'd rather tell a client the true thing than the comfortable one, and you want colleagues who do the same.

You think in corridors

India and the United States are one canvas to you, not two — and cross-border complexity energises rather than deters you.

You want range, not a lane

You'd rather move between advising, building, and investing than be confined to a single discipline for a decade.

Your judgment outruns your years

However senior your title, you bring real judgment — and you want to be trusted to use it, not managed by a pyramid.

You build to endure

You care that what you make keeps working after you step back — not merely that it shipped on time.

How we work

How we work.

Senior people on the actual work; candor as a default; a bias toward building the thing rather than describing it. We stay close to clients and to each other — small teams, real ownership, continuity over churn. We use AI to work faster and sharper, never as theatre, and we judge by output and judgment rather than face-time.

Because the work spans the corridor, there is genuine variety — markets, sectors, and problems that keep the craft sharp and the days unrepetitive. It is demanding, and it is meant to be; the reward is range, ownership, and proximity to consequence that larger firms cannot offer.
