What it is

Advisory and principal capital, on one corridor.

Aadi Capital is built around a simple asymmetry: most capital is either advice without conviction (a banker who shops your deck) or conviction without help (an investor who writes a cheque and watches). We hold both — and pair them with a consulting practice and a venture studio — so a company can be advised, prepared, introduced, funded, and operated by one accountable firm.

It runs as two arms. Capital Services is fee-based advisory: raise-readiness, financial engineering, the investment narrative, sell-side diligence, structuring, investor matchmaking, and transaction execution. Investing is principal — we take positions on our own thesis, from solopreneurs and early-stage through mid-market, family-business transitions, distressed turnarounds, special situations, and the structures that carry them.

Between advising and investing sits a real firewall — advisory serves the client; investing rests on our own documented thesis; confidences never cross. And across the whole spectrum, the corridor is the edge: warm relationships with capital and acquirers on both sides of India and the United States, run as a single market rather than two.

What makes the arm distinctive isn't a fund size we won't quote — it's that capital here is attached to operators. Where we believe, we don't just fund; we help build, through Consulting and Cofoundry. That is the difference between money and a partner.

The firewall between advice and capital.

A firm that both advises and invests carries one real risk — that an investment could be informed by, or compete with, a client. We treat it structurally. Advisory serves the client; investment rests on our own documented thesis; client confidences never cross; pricing between the arms is arm's-length; and conflicts are disclosed and reviewed before we act. Trust is the product, so we protect it by architecture.

How Capital connects

One conviction, three arms.

Capital is sharpest because it doesn't stand alone. Consulting surfaces the theses we back and prepares the companies we fund; Cofoundry turns the strongest into companies we co-own; and Errored Coin closes the cycle, recovering value and lessons from the situations that go wrong and feeding them back into how we judge the next one. A raise becomes a relationship; a relationship becomes a build; a build becomes a position — under one entity, with one accountable partner.

Questions

The things people ask first.

Are you an advisor or an investor?

Both, kept clean by a real firewall. Capital Services is fee advisory; Investing is principal capital on our own thesis.

Do you publish fund size or AUM?

No — we lead with thesis and method, not a number. The relevant question is whether we have conviction in your situation.

What is 'services-for-equity'?

Where cash is tight and conviction is real, part of our work converts to a founder-aligned stake instead of a full fee.

Can you introduce me to other investors?

Yes — warm introductions to fitting investors and acquirers on both sides of the corridor are core to Capital Services.

Might Aadi invest directly?

Where we believe, yes — and we bring operators through Consulting and Cofoundry to protect the position.

Raising, selling, or seeking conviction?

Tell us the situation. We'll tell you honestly whether it's a fee engagement, a principal position, or a company worth co-building.
